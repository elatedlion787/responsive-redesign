Redesigning Argren.Net

Argren.Net: A Norwegian Website In Need of Redesign

For this assignment for CS1300: UI/UX, I was tasked with finding and rebuilding a poorly-designed web UI page.

The website I have chosen to redesign is Argren.net, a Norwegian-based e-commerce website. Argren.net tries to emulate the look of a 'yellow-pages' type website, but struggles to do so in a cohesive way.

The website's layout is clunky, confusing and lacks basic structure or consistency. The design of the website is so inconvenient and unstructured, that I honestly believed it was a satirical website. The random information that is scattered throughout the page is hilarious and makes the website wonky to navigate.

For your curiosity (or to find the funny random items listed on the website), check out Argren.Net!



Part 1: Website Overview

Arngren.Net is a Norwegian e-commerce site that sells an assortment of random goods, ranging from electric vehicles to other electric items. It is actually unclear to tell what the company sells, because they have chosen to use a white-page layout, in which listings are not sorted, repeated and have inconsistent sizing, captions and text.

Furthermore, there seems to be very very random links across the page. For example, in one image, you can see a link for a request to produce a sci-fi movie, other requests to find partners for production, various electric vehicles and random logos and information cited throughout the page. When translating to English the page is even more difficult to navigate, because the inconsistent text makes the website characters difficult to read

To best illustrate the difficulty of using this interface, I will share a few screenshots of the website. Even as I do this project, I am still confused as to what the purpose of this site is (due to other pages, which aren't the focus of this project) being equally confusing

Evaluating Argren.net's Design

Usability Issues:

  • Layout: The website does not have a consistent layout and structure in terms of how items are sized, formatted or displayed on the screen. There is no cohension between elements on the screen, and it seems like elements have been randomly splattered on the screen
  • No Product Organization: Products are not organized outside of the left-handed directory, which doesn't seem to correlate with all the items on the main page
  • No Marking of Sold Out Products: Because these products are all listings (there are no details on when the listings are closed), you are able to view products (sometimes listed by third-parties) that have been already sold
  • Browser Fit: The website is not scaled for different devices, meaning it is incredibly difficult to view on devices with less screen space
  • Text Formatting: It is unclear which text belongs to which images
  • Repetition: There actually multiple pictures repeated across the screen. It is only difficult to notice due to the sheer range of items
  • Protruding Photos: Some photos are protuding off the pages
  • Lack of Theme/Cohesion: While the page seems to be a e-commerce site for technology and gadgets, there are a variety of links (such as a wifi-speed testing link, picture tiles, links to Playboy items and links to Craiglist-like listings looking for actors and directors) for products that don't fit the theme. These are not present on the left hand screen
  • Broken Components: The Search Bar, which is placed on two inconvenient places on the screen, does not actually work, making it hard to parse through the listings
  • Poor Translation: Due to the inconsistency of text sizing, image sizing and the general website layout, the English version does not scale particularly well
  • Inconsistent Colors: There are way too many colors used on the website, without a clear reason
  • Unclear Buttons: The main about page for the website is the logo on the top-left. However, this is not clear along with a few other clickable icons hidden on the screen

Accessibility Issues:

  • Usability: The usability of the website is horrendous. Not only is it impossible to search and index for specific products, it is fairly difficult to determine what information is referring to what image/product. Without a working search bar and a formatted webpage, I have struggled to find the user flow to go from seeing a product to understanding how to purchase a product. Without such fludity, it is almost impossible to see this website as even reasonbly functionable
  • Learnability: With no documentation or visible cohesion/format, it is impossible to learn how to use the interface. In the case new products are added, the user will have to completely re-learn the layout of the website, as its usability is dependent on knowing exactly where within the large screen (which doesn't fit in the browser), their item is.
  • Memorability: The website is fairly memorable, but largely because of its awful UI. The main elements I believe that preserve the website's character are it's aspects of randomness and weird layouts.When redesigning this UI, I don't want to get rid of all the randomness and weird quirks of the website, but I'd like to make it a minimal feature, rather than a critical building block of the website design.
  • Accessibility: The accessibility problems that WebAIM Wave brought up were:
    • Poor Contrast: Due to the inconsistency of colors and contrast used, some items have poor contrast between the text and background colors. There are also no consideration for colorblind users.
    • Text Sizing:Due to the lack of formatting with image and text sizing, some of the font-sizes used are too small for people with vision issues and will be difficult to navigate
    • No Alternative Text: Many of the images have no alternative text, making it very difficult for users who rely on such a feature, to use
